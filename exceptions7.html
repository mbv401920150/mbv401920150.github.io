Special Considerations for Outsourced Activities

The designation of activities as routine versus non-routine will continue to dictate the appropriate business authorization and accounting review limits needed for journal entries. Activities designated as routine will not require business authorization. Activities designated as non-routine will require business authorization and accounting reviews, with the following requirements:

  1. The outsourced partner should obtain the Business Transaction Authorizer and appropriate Corporate Accounting review (where required) based on the approval limits, prior to providing entries to GBS FSS.
  2. GBS FSS/HRSS owns ensuring the Accounting Transaction Owner review is obtained as these sign-offs are within GBS FSS/HRSS. As a part of this process, GBS FSS/HRSS should review the entries for overall reasonability and compliance with policy (e.g., appropriate documentation, appropriate authorization). GBS FSS/HRSS can reserve the right to reject an entry based on this review.

Routine entries that are systemically generated can be posted by the outsource partner. Routine entries that are manually generated should generally be posted by GBS FSS/HRSS, except when it is not practical, exceptions should be agreed with the G/L Service Line Manager and Corporate Accounting. All non-routine entries should be posted by GBS FSS/HRSS.
